     A REPLACEMENT FOR THE 6581/8580 SID CHIP: THE SwinSID

     If your SID (sound) chip has failed and you can't find a good 
replacement, there is an alternative. It's called the SwinSID. It's an 
early version and is still being sold as of this writing. The designer 
uses a different type of IC in an emulator arrangement which is close 
to the voicing of an original SID chip. There are several versions of 
the SwinSID available and they just plug in to the board like a "real"
SID chip. A later (smaller) version was called the Nano-SwinSID.
     One drawback of those modules which may not be mentioned is their 
lack of POT (potentiometer) inputs, specifically the two A/D converters 
built into the original SID chip. A proportional mouse, graphics tablet 
and paddle use those POT inputs to control the mouse pointer, etc. and 
will not work with a SwinSID. Note that a joystick doesn't use those 
inputs and works just fine without them.

     THE SwinSID ULTIMATE: A COMPLETE REWORK OF THE SwinSID design

     As of this writing, the newest SID replacement is SwinSID Ultimate
developed by someone in Hungary, and is being sold on Facebook. I have 
one and I can't tell the difference between it and an original SID chip.
It has the POT (mouse, paddle) inputs the previous versions lacked.
Since the designer is in Europe, the module defaults to PAL 8580 mode
but is easily programmable to reset it to NTSC 6581 with a small C64 
program. There is also an instruction manual that tells of the design 
and all the improvements from the early Swinkels version modules. 

NOTE: This module apparently has a problem with voicing when playing
some older game carts. "Clowns" is apparently one of them.
